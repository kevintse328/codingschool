Our Curriculum

Month 1-2: Programming Skills

The first weeks will cover web programming fundamentals such as HTML, Python, Javascript, Git, and the Terminal, skills which are in most demand.

Month 3-4: Capstone Project

Students will work with a real client to build an app. This will provide real-world experience collaborating with other programmers to solve business problems.

Month 5-6: Career Preparation

Students will learn and practice interview skills to prepare them as they go through the recruiting process with our partner companies.

Mission Statement

Refugees are too often just seen as a problem. Our goal at the Refugee Coding School is to show how our new neighbours can enrich our culture and empower our economy. Our goal is to help refugees develop their professional qualifications as programmers and software developers, and to provide them with the opportunities and support that is necessary to integrate successfully into our society.

Our Partners

Proudly based in Cologne.

Because the largest number of asylum seekers, approximately 196 000, migrated to Nordrhein-Westfalen in 2016, we decided to found our school in Cologne. As the fourth largest city in Germany, Cologne is a major economic center for banking, insurance, media, engineering and pharmaceuticals. Through partnerships with local companies, we plan to place new graduates of our program into jobs with companies who are in need of technically skilled labor.
